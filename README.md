# E-Commerce-Sales-Customer-Analysis-SQL

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing an E-commerce dataset to uncover insights related to sales performance, customer behavior, and business operations.
The data was originally provided in CSV format and transformed into a structured relational database using SQL Server to enable efficient querying and analysis.
The goal of this project is to simulate a real-world business scenario and demonstrate how SQL can be used to support data-driven decision-making.

## Business Problem

The management team lacks clear visibility into key business areas such as:

- Sales performance across products and regions.
- Customer purchasing behavior.
- Payment method preferences.
- Order status trends (Delivered, Cancelled, Pending).

This limits their ability to make informed decisions and optimize business performance.

## Project Objectives

- Analyze overall sales performance and trends.
- Identify top-performing products and categories.
- Understand customer behavior and purchasing patterns.
- Evaluate the impact of discounts on sales.
- Analyze order status distribution and cancellation rates.
- Compare performance across payment methods.

## Dataset Description

The dataset represents an E-commerce business and includes the following main entities:

- Orders: order details, status, discounts, payment methods.
- Customers: customer information and location.
- Products: product details and categories.

## Key Analysis & Insights

- 81% of orders were successfully delivered, indicating strong operational performance.
- 9% of orders remain pending, suggesting possible process delays.
- Cash is the most used payment method (47.3%), followed by Visa (42.3%).
- Instapay usage is low (10.4%), indicating a growth opportunity.
• Most orders have no discount, while some reach up to 20%.

## Business Impact
- Revenue is affected by cancellation rates and operational inefficiencies.
- High reliance on cash payments may slow operations and increase risk.
- Limited adoption of digital payments affects scalability.
- Discount strategies may not be fully optimized.

## Recommendations
- Improve order fulfillment processes to reduce cancellations.
- Encourage digital payments through targeted incentives.
- Optimize handling of pending orders to improve customer experience.
- Apply data-driven discount strategies instead of random usage.
